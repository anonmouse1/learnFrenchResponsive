Listen

1. Listen to a French podcast every morning

Most people can spare 10-15 minutes in the morning while making and eating breakfast to listen to a podcast. Take advantage of your deadtime and passively pick up words and phrases without having to make any effort, it can simply be background noise while you butter your croissant. That's right, strictly croissants from now on for breakfast, you are serious about learning French aren't you ?

Recommend: Francais Authentique

Listen again......

2. Listen to French radio or music during the day.

On the bus, in the car, some form of French audio can be found 24/7 so you will never be stuck for some iconoclastic songs or news about the latest destruction of Paris by striking pensioners. If you don't like the radio, you can always listen to music.

Recommend: Radio FIP

Watch

3. Watch an episode of a French TV show.

This one should be easy for most people, yes, in this case watching TV is a very productive use of your time. Use subtitles (in your own language at first, then move to French subtitles only) and then turn them off completely when you feel you understand 80% or more. (Note:You may need a French VPN to access some of these shows).

Recommend: Un si grand soleil

Read

4. Read one page of a french book

Reading is a great way to learn new words and phrases. It is the necessary complement to listening, even if it is a not so passive way of learning. This is why I recommend only aiming to achieve one page per day, if you read more then great but you are not "burdened" with needing to read a full chapter when all you want to do is go to dreamland. An excellent tip from a reddit user.

(PS my number one tip for reading in French: don't look up every word you don't know! Just soldier through. If you see a word more than 5 times and you still haven't figured it out from context, look it up. Otherwise, just keep reading. It will go faster; you will enjoy yourself more; and your brain will catch on, making you learn even more.)Source

Recommend: L'Etranger by Albert Camus

TLDR;

  • Morning: Listen to a french podcast episode (Recommend: Francais Authentique)
  • Daytime/deadtime: Listen to french radio or music (Recommend: Radio Fip via TuneIn)
  • Evening/deadtime: Watch an episode of a french tv show (Recommend: Un si grand soleil via france.tv)
  • Bedtime: Read one or more pages of a french book
